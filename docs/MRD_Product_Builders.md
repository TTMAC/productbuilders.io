## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Problem Hypothesis

Product builders (designers, engineers, and product managers) struggle to develop holistic product thinking skills because existing knowledge resources are siloed by discipline. This results in cross-functional friction, longer alignment cycles, and products that fail to balance technical feasibility, user desirability, and business viability.

The rise of AI-augmented product development and increasingly complex digital products has intensified the need for T-shaped professionals who understand adjacent disciplines. Yet 78% of product knowledge content remains single-discipline focused, leaving a significant gap in the market for integrated, cross-functional learning.

### 1.2 Target Job Executor

Mid-career product professionals (3-8 years experience) at technology companies who are transitioning into leadership roles or seeking to expand their influence across functions. These individuals have mastered their core discipline but recognize that career advancement and product excellence require understanding the constraints, goals, and mental models of their cross-functional partners.

### 1.3 Proposed Value Proposition

ProductBuilders.io is the first content platform designed explicitly for cross-functional product literacy. Unlike discipline-specific blogs (UX Collective for designers, Dev.to for engineers, Lenny's Newsletter for PMs), we deliver integrated perspectives where every article surfaces how design, engineering, and product decisions interconnect. Readers develop the shared vocabulary and mutual understanding that transforms functional collaboration from conflict to co-creation.

### 1.4 Success Criteria

- Achieve 25,000 monthly active readers within 12 months of launch
- Attain newsletter open rate >45% (vs. industry average of 21%)
- Generate NPS >50 from reader surveys
- 40% of content engagement from readers outside their primary discipline
- Establish sustainable monetization path (sponsorship or premium tier) by month 18

### 1.5 Investment Ask

**Phase 1 (MVP):** 3-month timeline leveraging Claude Code for accelerated development

- **Development:** Tshepo Machele + Claude Code partnership (AI-assisted full-stack development)
- **Content:** Tshepo Machele as founding editor with cross-functional perspective
- **Infrastructure:** Minimal hosting costs (~$50/month for blog + newsletter platform)

**Phase 2 (Growth):** Additional content contributors from each discipline, community features

**Key milestones:** MVP launch (Month 3), 5K subscribers (Month 6), monetization pilot (Month 12)

The Claude Code development approach dramatically reduces traditional engineering costs and enables rapid iteration based on user feedback.

## 3. Job Executor Profile

### 3.1 Primary Job Executor

Mid-career product professionals (designers, engineers, product managers) with 3-8 years of experience at technology companies ranging from growth-stage startups to large enterprises. They have achieved competence in their core discipline and are now facing the ceiling that comes from functional expertise alone.

These individuals are often preparing for or recently promoted to senior/lead roles where cross-functional influence is essential. They recognize that their career advancement depends not just on deeper technical skills, but on their ability to communicate, collaborate, and make decisions with partners from other disciplines.

### 3.2 Circumstances of Struggle

The struggling moment occurs most intensely during:

- **Sprint planning disagreements** where technical constraints clash with design vision or business priorities
- **Product reviews** where stakeholders from different functions talk past each other using discipline-specific jargon
- **Career development conversations** where managers emphasize 'stakeholder management' without providing actionable guidance
- **New job onboarding** where understanding the company's cross-functional dynamics is critical but undocumented

The frustration peaks when professionals realize they're repeating the same alignment battles project after project, wasting cycles that could be spent building better products.

### 3.3 Current Alternatives (Solutions "Hired" Today)

| Alternative | What It Does Well | Where It Falls Short | % Using |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------------|---------|
| Do nothing / learn on the job | No cost; real-world context | Slow, painful, relationship damage from mistakes | ~40% |
| Discipline-specific blogs/newsletters | Deep expertise; strong communities | Single-function perspective; reinforces silos | ~35% |
| Books on collaboration | Comprehensive frameworks | Generic; not product-specific; time-intensive | ~15% |
| Formal training/courses | Structured learning; credentials | Expensive ($500-2K+); time commitment; still often siloed | ~10% |

## 8. Minimum Viable Product Definition

### 8.1 MVP Scope

The MVP is a weekly newsletter + blog delivering one high-quality article per week that explicitly addresses cross-functional product building. Each article will include perspectives from at least two disciplines and end with actionable takeaways for readers from each function.

**Technical Implementation (via Claude Code):**

- Static site generator (Astro/Next.js) for fast, SEO-optimized blog
- Newsletter integration (Buttondown/ConvertKit) for subscriber management
- Simple analytics (Plausible/Fathom) for engagement tracking
- Deployed on Vercel/Netlify for zero-ops hosting

**The complete experience:** Subscribe ‚Üí Receive weekly email with featured article ‚Üí Read on simple, fast-loading blog ‚Üí Optionally share or discuss on social media.

**What readers will accomplish that they couldn't before:** Gain insight into how their cross-functional partners think about the same problems, delivered in a format designed for time-constrained professionals (10-minute reads).

### 8.2 Target Outcomes for MVP

| Outcome Statement | In MVP? | Rationale |
|-------------------|---------|-----------|
| Minimize time to understand cross-functional pushback | ‚òí Yes ‚òê No | Core value prop‚Äîevery article addresses this |
| Minimize likelihood of alienating jargon | ‚òí Yes ‚òê No | Editorial standard: no unexplained jargon |
| Maximize ability to anticipate constraints | ‚òí Yes ‚òê No | Scenario-based articles with multi-function views |
| Minimize effort to find relevant content | ‚òê Yes ‚òí No | Phase 2: content library with filtering |
| Minimize alignment meetings required | ‚òê Yes ‚òí No | Indirect outcome; hard to measure in MVP |

### 8.3 Target Customer for MVP

Our first 1,000 readers will be mid-career product professionals at Series B-D startups who:

- Have 4-7 years of experience in their primary discipline
- Work on cross-functional product teams (not siloed organizations)
- Already subscribe to 2+ professional newsletters (established learning habit)
- Are active on Twitter/LinkedIn discussing product topics
- Have experienced recent cross-functional friction they can recall specifically

These early adopters will love the MVP despite limited content library because the cross-functional framing immediately resonates with their daily struggles. They'll forgive rough edges for differentiated insight.

## 9. Success Metrics & Milestones

### 9.1 North Star Metric

> **Weekly Active Readers Who Engage Across Disciplines**

**Definition:** Unique readers who, in a given week, either (a) open and read an article from a discipline other than their primary, or (b) engage (comment, share, reply) with cross-functional content.

This captures both adoption (active usage) and our unique value delivery (cross-functional engagement). If we grow readers but they only consume content from their own discipline, we've failed to deliver on our differentiation.

### 9.2 Key Metrics by Stage

| Stage | Key Metrics | Target |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Discovery** | Problem validation interviews; landing page signup rate | 20 interviews; >5% landing page conversion |
| **Validation** | Newsletter open rate; article completion rate; NPS | >45% open rate; >60% completion; NPS >40 |
| **Growth** | Subscriber growth; cross-discipline engagement; referral rate | 25K subscribers by M12; 40% cross-discipline; 15% referral |

### 9.3 Key Milestones

## 10. Out of Scope

### 10.1 Explicitly Excluded from MVP

| What's Excluded | Rationale |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Community forum / discussion platform | Requires critical mass of content + users first; revisit at 10K subscribers |
| Paid courses or premium content tier | Must prove free content value before asking for payment; revisit Month 12 |
| Podcast or video content | Higher production cost; text-first validates core value prop more efficiently |
| Job board or career services | Distraction from core mission; only consider if strong inbound demand |
| Non-English content | Focus on English market first; localization after proving model |

### 10.2 Future Considerations

The following may be in scope for future iterations:

- **Content library with search/filtering** by discipline, topic, and scenario (trigger: >50 published articles)
- **Community Slack or Discord** (trigger: >10K subscribers and inbound requests)
- **Live events / AMAs** with cross-functional leaders (trigger: sponsor interest or community demand)
- **Team subscriptions** for product organizations (trigger: inbound enterprise inquiries)
- **Contributor network** to scale content beyond founding team (trigger: content quality sustainable at 1x/week)
- **AI-powered content recommendations** using Claude API (trigger: sufficient content volume)
